--- WHAT QUALIFIES AS AN EMERGENCY ---

GO TO EMERGENCY ROOM IMMEDIATELY FOR:
  🚨 Chest pain or suspected heart attack
  🚨 Stroke symptoms (Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty – FAST)
  🚨 Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness
  🚨 Severe difficulty breathing
  🚨 Severe head injury or trauma
  🚨 Heavy uncontrolled bleeding
  🚨 Seizures / fits
  🚨 Suspected poisoning or drug overdose
  🚨 Severe burns
  🚨 Road traffic accidents
  🚨 Sudden paralysis or numbness on one side
  🚨 Choking
  🚨 Suspected fracture with severe pain / deformity
  🚨 Anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction with swelling of throat)
  🚨 Suicidal ideation or attempt
  🚨 High fever in newborn or infant (under 3 months)
  🚨 Difficulty urinating with severe pain
  🚨 Sudden loss of vision

--- FIRST AID GUIDANCE WHILE WAITING FOR HELP ---

HEART ATTACK (Suspected):
  - Call 108 / ambulance immediately
  - Keep patient calm, seated or lying down
  - Loosen tight clothing
  - If unconscious and not breathing, begin CPR
  - Do NOT give food or water
  - Do NOT leave the patient alone

STROKE (FAST Check):
  - Face: Ask to smile – is one side drooping?
  - Arms: Ask to raise both – does one drift down?
  - Speech: Ask to repeat a phrase – is it slurred?
  - Time: Call 108 IMMEDIATELY – every minute matters
  - Do NOT give aspirin (unless confirmed by doctor)
  - Do NOT let the person eat or drink

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) – Basic:
  - Lay person on flat surface, on their back
  - Tilt head back, lift chin
  - 30 chest compressions: Push hard and fast in center of chest
  - 2 rescue breaths (if trained)
  - Continue until ambulance arrives or person responds
  Note: Hands-only CPR (30 compressions only) is acceptable if untrained in rescue breaths.

CHOKING:
  - If coughing, encourage them to keep coughing
  - If unable to cough or breathe: Stand behind, give 5 back blows (between shoulder blades)
  - Then 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver) – fist above navel, pull inward-upward
  - Alternate until object comes out or patient loses consciousness
  - If unconscious: start CPR, call 108

BLEEDING:
  - Apply firm direct pressure with clean cloth
  - Do NOT remove cloth if it soaks through – add more layers
  - Elevate the injured area above the heart if possible
  - Do NOT use a tourniquet unless trained; in life-threatening limb bleed, use one
  - Call ambulance for severe bleeding

BURNS:
  - Cool the burn with cool (not ice-cold) running water for 10–20 minutes
  - Remove rings, watches from affected area
  - Do NOT apply butter, oil, or toothpaste
  - Cover loosely with cling film or clean non-fluffy cloth
  - Go to Emergency for burns covering more than the palm size

SEIZURE:
  - Stay calm
  - Protect from injury – remove sharp objects nearby
  - Place person on their side (recovery position)
  - Do NOT hold the person down
  - Do NOT put anything in their mouth
  - Time the seizure; call 108 if it lasts more than 5 minutes

POISONING / OVERDOSE:
  - Call Poison Control: 1800-116-117
  - Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by poison control
  - Bring the substance bottle / label to hospital
  - Keep patient awake and talking if conscious
  - Rush to Emergency

SEVERE ALLERGIC REACTION (Anaphylaxis):
  - Signs: Throat swelling, difficulty breathing, rash, drop in blood pressure
  - Use EpiPen if prescribed and available
  - Call 108 immediately
  - Lay person flat; raise their legs unless breathing is difficult
