--- TEST PREPARATION INSTRUCTIONS ---

BLOOD TESTS REQUIRING FASTING:
  - Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS): Fast for 8–10 hours. Only water is allowed.
  - Lipid Profile: Fast for 9–12 hours. Avoid fatty food the night before.
  - HbA1c: No fasting required.
  - Liver Function Test (LFT): Fast for 8 hours preferred (not strictly mandatory).
  - Kidney Function Test: No fasting required.
  - Thyroid Profile (T3, T4, TSH): No fasting required. Best done in the morning.
  - Complete Blood Count (CBC): No fasting required.
  - Vitamin D3 / B12: No fasting required.
  - Insulin Fasting: Fast for 8 hours.
  - Cortisol (morning): No fasting; ideally collected between 8–10 AM.

URINE TESTS:
  - Urine Routine & Microscopy: First morning urine (mid-stream sample) is preferred.
  - 24-Hour Urine Collection:
      Step 1: Discard the first morning urine.
      Step 2: Collect ALL urine for the next 24 hours in the container provided.
      Step 3: Include the first morning urine of the second day.
      Step 4: Keep container refrigerated.
  - Urine Pregnancy Test (hCG): First morning urine gives most accurate result.
  - Urine Culture: Mid-stream clean-catch sample in sterile container.

STOOL TESTS:
  - Stool Routine & Culture: Fresh sample (less than 2 hours old) in sterile container.
  - H. Pylori Stool Antigen: Fresh sample; avoid antibiotics/PPIs for 2 weeks before test.
  - Avoid barium tests 7 days before stool tests.

CULTURE TESTS (Blood, Urine, Wound):
  - Blood Culture: Ideally collected at fever spike; 3 samples from different sites.
  - Avoid antibiotics before culture if possible (inform doctor).

--- SPECIFIC TEST INSTRUCTIONS ---

ECG (Electrocardiogram):
  - No special preparation needed
  - Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  - Avoid oily skin creams on chest area on the day of test
  - Inform technician of any pacemaker or metal implants

Echocardiography (Echo):
  - No fasting required
  - Wear loose clothing
  - Avoid applying powder or lotion on chest

Treadmill Test (TMT / Stress Test):
  - Wear comfortable shoes and clothing suitable for walking/running
  - Do NOT eat 3 hours before the test (light snack allowed)
  - Avoid caffeine and smoking 4 hours before
  - Inform doctor of all medications – some may be paused before TMT
  - Bring all current medicines list
  - Test takes approximately 30–45 minutes

CT Scan:
  - Abdominal CT: Fast for 4 hours before if contrast is used
  - CT Head: No preparation needed unless contrast required
  - CT with contrast (dye): Blood creatinine test required first (check kidney function)
  - Inform technician if: pregnant, diabetic (on Metformin), allergic to iodine/shellfish
  - Remove metallic objects, jewelry before scan
  - Scan takes 5–15 minutes

MRI:
  - No radiation; uses magnetic field
  - Fast for 4 hours if abdominal/pelvic MRI with contrast
  - MUST inform about: pacemakers, cochlear implants, aneurysm clips, metal implants
  - Remove ALL metallic objects, cards, hearing aids, jewelry
  - No makeup/cosmetics (some contain metallic particles)
  - Patients with claustrophobia should inform us in advance (mild sedation can be arranged)
  - Scan takes 30–60 minutes depending on body part
  - Wear loose, comfortable clothing

Ultrasound:
  - Abdomen USG: Fast for 6–8 hours (water only allowed)
  - Pelvis USG: Full bladder needed – drink 4–6 glasses of water 1 hour before; DO NOT urinate
  - Obstetric USG (pregnancy): Full bladder for first trimester; empty bladder for later trimesters
  - Thyroid / Neck USG: No preparation required
  - No preparation for breast or musculoskeletal ultrasound

Endoscopy (Upper GI):
  - Strict fasting: No food or drink for 6–8 hours before procedure
  - Inform doctor of blood thinners (aspirin, warfarin) – may need to stop
  - You will need a companion to take you home (sedation is given)
  - Wear comfortable clothes
  - The procedure takes 15–30 minutes

Colonoscopy:
  - Bowel preparation (laxative) required the day before – we will provide a preparation kit
  - Clear liquid diet the day before (water, clear broth, apple juice – no red or purple)
  - Nothing by mouth 6 hours before procedure
  - Blood thinners may need to be paused (consult doctor)
  - You need a companion to accompany you home
  - Procedure takes 30–60 minutes

Pap Smear:
  - Avoid sexual intercourse 2 days before
  - Do not use vaginal creams, douches, or tampons 2 days before
  - Ideally done mid-cycle (10–20 days after period start)
  - Not done during menstruation

--- REPORT COLLECTION & DELIVERY ---

How to Collect Reports:
  1. Counter: Lab Report Counter, Ground Floor, Wing C (7 AM – 8 PM)
  2. Online: Download from www.cityhospital.in/reports (Patient ID + Date of Birth)
  3. WhatsApp: Reports sent automatically to registered mobile number
  4. Email: Request at lab reception for email delivery

Report Turnaround Times:
  Routine Blood Tests:    4–6 hours
  Urine / Stool:         Same day
  Culture Reports:        24–72 hours
  Thyroid / Hormones:    Same day to next day
  CT / X-Ray:            2–4 hours (images + report)
  MRI:                   Same day to 24 hours
  Histopathology/Biopsy: 5–7 working days
  Genetic Tests:          2–3 weeks

Normal Value Reference Ranges (on report):
  - All reports include reference ranges
  - Values outside range are marked HIGH (H) or LOW (L) in red
  - Borderline values are flagged separately
  - A doctor must interpret your report; ranges alone do not constitute a diagnosis
