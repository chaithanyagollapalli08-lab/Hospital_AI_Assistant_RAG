--- COMMON SYMPTOMS & GUIDANCE ---

CHEST PAIN
Possible Causes: Heart attack, angina, acid reflux (GERD), muscle strain, anxiety, pulmonary embolism
Warning Signs (EMERGENCY – Call 108 immediately):
  - Crushing or pressure-like pain spreading to arm, jaw, neck, or back
  - Chest pain with sweating, nausea, or breathlessness
  - Sudden severe chest pain in a young person
General Guidance:
  - Do not ignore chest pain, even if mild
  - Avoid self-medicating
  - Sit or lie down; avoid physical exertion
Consult: Cardiologist or Emergency Department immediately
Disclaimer: Chest pain can be life-threatening. Please seek medical attention right away.

---

BREATHLESSNESS / SHORTNESS OF BREATH
Possible Causes: Asthma, COPD, pneumonia, heart failure, anemia, anxiety
Warning Signs (EMERGENCY):
  - Unable to speak in full sentences
  - Blue lips or fingertips
  - Breathlessness at rest in elderly or heart patients
General Guidance:
  - Sit upright, stay calm
  - Use inhaler if prescribed (for known asthmatics)
  - Avoid dusty or smoky environments
Consult: Pulmonologist, Cardiologist, or Emergency
Disclaimer: Seek medical attention promptly for unexplained breathlessness.

---

FEVER
Normal Temperature: Below 98.6°F (37°C)
Low-Grade Fever: 99°F – 100.4°F
High Fever: Above 103°F (39.4°C)
Possible Causes: Viral infection, bacterial infection, dengue, malaria, typhoid, COVID-19, UTI
Warning Signs (Seek immediate care):
  - Fever above 104°F
  - Fever with severe headache and stiff neck (possible meningitis)
  - Fever with rash
  - Fever in infants under 3 months
  - Fever lasting more than 3 days
General Guidance:
  - Stay hydrated – drink plenty of fluids
  - Rest adequately
  - Paracetamol (as directed) to reduce temperature
  - Do not use aspirin in children
  - Cool compresses on forehead
Consult: General Physician
Disclaimer: Consult a doctor before taking any medication.

---

HEADACHE
Types: Tension headache, migraine, cluster headache, sinus headache
Possible Causes: Stress, dehydration, eye strain, hypertension, sinusitis
Warning Signs (EMERGENCY):
  - Sudden "thunderclap" headache (worst of life – possible brain hemorrhage)
  - Headache with fever, stiff neck, rash
  - Headache with vision changes, confusion, weakness
General Guidance:
  - Rest in a quiet, dark room
  - Stay hydrated
  - Avoid screen time during severe headache
  - Cold/warm compress on forehead or neck
Consult: Neurologist (for recurrent/severe headaches), General Physician
Disclaimer: Sudden severe headache is a medical emergency. Seek care immediately.

---

ABDOMINAL PAIN
Possible Causes: Gastritis, appendicitis, kidney stones, gallstones, IBS, constipation, food poisoning
Warning Signs (EMERGENCY):
  - Sudden severe abdominal pain
  - Pain with fever and vomiting
  - Rigid/hard abdomen
  - Blood in stool or vomit
General Guidance:
  - Avoid solid foods if nauseous; sip clear fluids
  - Do not take painkillers without diagnosis (can mask symptoms)
  - Track location, severity, and pattern of pain
Consult: Gastroenterologist, General Physician, or Emergency for severe pain
Disclaimer: Do not delay care for severe or persistent abdominal pain.

---

DIABETES – BASICS
Type 1: Body does not produce insulin (auto-immune)
Type 2: Body does not use insulin effectively (most common)
Common Symptoms: Excessive thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision, slow wound healing
Normal Blood Sugar Ranges:
  - Fasting: 70–100 mg/dL
  - Post-meal (2hr): Less than 140 mg/dL
  - HbA1c: Below 5.7% (normal), 5.7–6.4% (pre-diabetic), 6.5%+ (diabetic)
Management Tips:
  - Follow diabetic diet: Low sugar, high fiber
  - Regular exercise (30 min daily)
  - Take medication as prescribed
  - Monitor blood sugar regularly
  - Regular foot, eye, and kidney checkups
Warning Signs (Hypoglycemia – Low Sugar Emergency):
  - Sweating, trembling, confusion, loss of consciousness
  - Give sugar immediately (juice, glucose) if conscious
Consult: Endocrinologist / Diabetologist
Disclaimer: Diabetes management requires personalized medical supervision.

---

HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE)
Normal BP: Less than 120/80 mmHg
Elevated: 120-129 / less than 80 mmHg
Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139 / 80-89 mmHg
Stage 2 Hypertension: 140+ / 90+ mmHg
Crisis (EMERGENCY): Above 180/120 mmHg
Symptoms (often none – "Silent Killer"): Occasional headache, nosebleed, blurred vision
Management:
  - Reduce salt intake
  - Exercise regularly
  - Maintain healthy weight
  - Avoid alcohol and smoking
  - Take prescribed medication consistently
  - Monitor BP at home
Consult: Cardiologist, General Physician
Disclaimer: Hypertension requires consistent medical follow-up.

---

COMMON COLD vs FLU
Cold: Gradual onset, runny nose, mild fever, sore throat
Flu: Sudden onset, high fever, body aches, extreme fatigue, headache
Treatment:
  - Rest and fluids
  - Paracetamol for fever/aches
  - Steam inhalation for congestion
  - Flu may require antiviral medication (consult doctor)
When to See Doctor:
  - High fever for more than 3 days
  - Difficulty breathing
  - Worsening symptoms after initial improvement
Consult: General Physician

---

SKIN RASH
Possible Causes: Allergic reaction, eczema, psoriasis, fungal infection, chickenpox, dengue
Warning Signs (Seek care):
  - Rash with fever and difficulty breathing (possible anaphylaxis)
  - Rapidly spreading rash
  - Rash with joint pain
General Guidance:
  - Do not scratch rash
  - Avoid self-applying steroid creams without prescription
  - Keep the area clean and dry
  - Note if rash appeared after starting a new medication
Consult: Dermatologist
Disclaimer: Rash with breathing difficulty is an emergency. Call 108 immediately.

---

JOINT PAIN
Possible Causes: Arthritis (osteo or rheumatoid), gout, injury, lupus, infection in joint
Red Flags:
  - Joint swelling with fever (possible septic arthritis)
  - Morning stiffness lasting more than an hour
  - Joint pain in multiple joints with fatigue (possible rheumatoid arthritis)
General Guidance:
  - Rest the affected joint
  - Ice pack for acute injury (first 48 hours)
  - Warm compress for chronic aching
  - Avoid high-impact activities
Consult: Orthopedic Surgeon, Rheumatologist

---

DIARRHEA & VOMITING
Possible Causes: Food poisoning, viral gastroenteritis, cholera, rotavirus
General Guidance:
  - Stay well hydrated – ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts) recommended
  - Avoid dairy, oily, or spicy food
  - BRAT diet: Banana, Rice, Applesauce, Toast
  - Wash hands frequently
Warning Signs (Seek care):
  - Blood in stool or vomit
  - Dehydration (no urine for 8+ hours, dry mouth, dizziness)
  - High fever with diarrhea
  - Symptoms in infants, elderly, or pregnant women
Consult: General Physician, Gastroenterologist
