--- VISITING HOURS POLICY ---

General Wards:
  Morning: 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM
  Evening: 5:00 PM – 7:00 PM
  Maximum visitors per patient: 2 at a time
  Children under 12: Not permitted in wards (infection control)

Private / Deluxe Rooms:
  Morning: 8:00 AM – 11:00 AM
  Evening: 4:00 PM – 8:00 PM
  Maximum visitors: 3 at a time

ICU / NICU / HDU (High Dependency Unit):
  Visiting Hours: 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM | 5:00 PM – 6:00 PM
  Persons allowed: 1 immediate family member only per session
  Children under 18: NOT permitted in ICU
  Visitor gowns/masks required (provided at ICU entrance)

Post-Operative Ward:
  No visitors for first 4 hours post-surgery
  After that: 1 visitor at a time, during general visiting hours

Emergency Department:
  Only 1 family member permitted to be inside the Emergency Department at a time
  Others must wait in the designated family waiting area

COVID-19 / Isolation Wards:
  Visiting strictly restricted
  Video call facility arranged by nursing staff

--- PATIENT RIGHTS ---

Every patient has the right to:
  ✅ Respectful and dignified care without discrimination
  ✅ Privacy and confidentiality of medical information
  ✅ Know the name and designation of their treating doctor and nurse
  ✅ Informed consent before any procedure or surgery
  ✅ Refuse treatment after being informed of consequences
  ✅ Access their medical records on written request
  ✅ Receive a complete and itemized bill
  ✅ Seek a second opinion
  ✅ Lodge a complaint without fear of retribution
  ✅ Be informed about expected costs before treatment

--- PATIENT RESPONSIBILITIES ---

Patients are expected to:
  ✅ Provide accurate and complete medical history
  ✅ Follow prescribed treatment and instructions
  ✅ Treat hospital staff respectfully
  ✅ Keep appointments or cancel in advance
  ✅ Settle bills promptly
  ✅ Respect other patients' privacy and quiet
  ✅ Not bring prohibited items (alcohol, outside food in ICU, weapons)
  ✅ Follow visitor rules and inform visitors of hospital policies

--- ADMISSION POLICY ---

Planned Admissions:
  - Doctor's admission note is mandatory
  - Admission Desk: Ground Floor, near Main Reception
  - Identity Proof: Aadhar card, Passport, or Voter ID (Mandatory)
  - Deposit: Refundable security deposit required at the time of admission
      General Ward: ₹5,000
      Semi-Private: ₹10,000
      Private Room: ₹15,000
      Deluxe Suite: ₹25,000
  - Balance of deposit returned at discharge after settling all dues

Emergency Admissions:
  - No prior documentation required to begin treatment
  - Documents to be submitted within 24 hours of admission
  - If patient is unidentified: Treatment initiated; helpline and police informed

--- DISCHARGE POLICY ---

Discharge Process:
  Step 1: Treating doctor issues discharge order (usually in the morning)
  Step 2: Nursing team prepares discharge summary and medication list
  Step 3: Billing department prepares final bill (allow 2–4 hours)
  Step 4: Patient / family reviews and settles the bill
  Step 5: Pharmacy provides discharge medications
  Step 6: Collect original discharge summary, reports, and prescriptions
  Step 7: Security clears patient at gate upon showing discharge slip

Discharge Timing:
  Preferred Discharge Time: By 12:00 PM (noon)
  Late Discharge (after 12 PM): Half-day room charges applicable
  Very Late Discharge (after 6 PM): Full-day room charges applicable

LAMA (Leave Against Medical Advice):
  - Patient or family can choose to leave even if doctor advises against it
  - LAMA form must be signed by patient/guardian
  - Hospital is not responsible for consequences after LAMA

--- REFUND POLICY ---

Advance / Deposit Refund:
  - Refunded at discharge after deducting all dues
  - Refund Mode: Same as payment mode (cash returns cash; card/UPI returns to original account)
  - Processing: Immediate at billing counter

Appointment Cancellation Refund:
  - Cancelled 2+ hours before: Full refund
  - Late cancellation: ₹50 deducted
  - Online refund to original payment method: 5–7 working days
  - Counter payment refund: Same day at billing counter

Package Refund:
  - Health packages: Refundable if cancelled before any test is done (minus ₹100 admin fee)
  - Partially used packages: Non-refundable for used components; balance may be transferred

Surgery/Procedure Cancellation:
  - Cancelled 48+ hours before: Full refund of advance
  - Cancelled within 48 hours: 25% of advance deducted as cancellation charges
  - Cancelled on the day of procedure: 50% deducted
  - Cancellation due to medical reasons: Full refund after review

--- NO-SMOKING / NO-ALCOHOL POLICY ---

City Hospital is a NO SMOKING, NO ALCOHOL, NO TOBACCO zone.
Smoking (including e-cigarettes) is strictly prohibited anywhere on hospital premises.
Violators may be asked to leave the premises.
This applies to patients, visitors, attendants, and staff.

--- MOBILE PHONE POLICY ---

Mobile phones are permitted in most areas.
Phones must be on SILENT in all wards, OPDs, and procedure rooms.
Mobile usage is STRICTLY PROHIBITED in:
  - ICU / NICU
  - Operation Theatres
  - MRI Rooms (metallic interference with machine)
  - Neonatal Units

--- PHOTOGRAPHY & VIDEOGRAPHY ---

Photography of patients WITHOUT consent is strictly prohibited.
Recording medical procedures without written consent of the doctor is prohibited.
Security cameras are installed throughout the hospital for patient and staff safety.

--- COMPLAINT & GRIEVANCE POLICY ---

How to Raise a Complaint:
  - Speak with the Nursing Supervisor (Ward Level)
  - Speak with the Patient Experience Manager (Ground Floor, Reception)
  - Email: feedback@cityhospital.in
  - Fill Complaint Form available at any nursing station or reception
  - Online form: www.cityhospital.in/feedback

Resolution Timeline:
  - Acknowledgement: Within 24 hours
  - Resolution: Within 7 working days for most issues
  - Complex complaints: Within 15 working days
  - Escalation to Hospital Director: Available if not resolved

--- MEDICAL RECORDS POLICY ---

Patients may request their medical records in writing.
Allowed requests: Discharge summary, investigation reports, operation notes.
Processing Time: 3–5 working days.
Records Department: 1st Floor, Main Building.
Records are kept for 7 years as per Indian law.
Electronic copies can be emailed on written request.

--- CHILD SAFETY POLICY ---

Children under 12 are not permitted in general wards or ICU.
Children must be accompanied by a guardian at all times.
Pediatric ward is restricted to patient and 2 guardians only.
Photography of children without parental consent is strictly prohibited.

--- INFECTION CONTROL POLICY ---

All visitors must sanitize hands at entry.
Hand sanitizer dispensers are placed at all ward entrances.
Masks are mandatory for visitors in ICU, NICU, and Oncology wards.
Patients showing signs of contagious illness may be placed in isolation.
No outside food is permitted in ICU.
